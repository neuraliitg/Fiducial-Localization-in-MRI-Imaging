Launching your own Personal Website

The Easy and the hard way.

Dispatching your personal website is the best way to set your footsteps in web development. Though a bit time consuming, but something definitely worth trying.

14 January, 2018



The art of making websites - Web Development or often we give it a cool slang WebD, is a fascinating domain. The amazing creativity of designers and developers often lead us to wonder how these sites are developed and what actually goes inside a typical website. It often leads us to wonder if this technology could serve us to cater our interests as well, but at a zero monetary cost(Remember! nothing is for free. The time you invest in such activities also possess some value). Well, this is not difficult as it looks like. Except graphics, everything else in the web development can be learned with slight effort, even if you are not good at programming.


In this article, I try to address these questions in a sequential order. I will try to explain the background behind the steps. Rest of all depends upon your creativity and ability to learn the web development languages(in case you want to code it from scratch.)



Why a personal website??


The reasons you need a website can be subjective. It depends upon the individual and specific interests at that point of time, but it boils down to increase your online presence. If people can find you easily over the internet, your prospects of getting hired are boosted. It depends on an individual how he uses this online presence to his own advantage. The inception of the internet has led to the human existance as slightly multidimensional - one in real world and other in the cyberspace. The phenomenon is evident in case of social media and micro-blogging sites. We spend most our time on these platforms. But how often do we realise their inability to provide a positive online presence.


A personal website is what can provide you with a professional outlook in terms of online presence. Your social presence on Facebook means nothing to your recruiter. Also, these platforms are restricted with a fixed interface. Your website provides you with the liberty to choose the way you want to represent yourself.


Maintaining your portfolio on your website provides a kind of validity to the content in your Resume. Your resume will be restricted to 1-2 pages but the website can be a further description of the projects that you have done.


You can check out Thomas Frank's website for the reference. This guy has maintained an amazing website with the collection of all of his portfolios.



Online resumes are quite popular these days. Their relevance may vary from person to person.

Getting Started - How the hell website works??


Understanding how a a static website functions is quite simple. All you need is these 3 guys - one for hosting your website(webhost server) and keeping your content, second guy is for keeping the record of your website name aka DNS server(Domain Name System), and the last one, don't think it's you, who can write the relevant content for the website.


Starting it from the web server, this is where your website content will be stored and the guy who will maintain this computer will be your web-host. They charge you some fees for maintaining the servers and keeping your site up. But as I promised a free alternative, I will be describing the steps to get it done without spending a dollar.


Getting the Web Host - For free


Well, everything comes at a cost and so, the nice guys who can provide you with the web-hosting services at a compromise on the desired domain name are available. Now there is a choice. As I wrote in subtitle, there is an easy way and a hard way. Both of them come with some benefits and some losses. It depends upon what you will prefer.

The Easy way


If you want to develop the content a bit easy way, go for sites which provides a MS Word type of interface for editing the webpages. One such example is Weebly. Weebly has lots of features and templates to use. But again! a restricted workspace and less scope for creativity. You cannot add extra features without spending some bucks out of your wallet(Something, you definitely, don't want).


The steps are simple. Register on the Weebly and choose create a site option in the menu.


Once you are done with the editing part, click on publish site. The site will ask you for your preferred free domain name. Write the desired domain name and click Continue. Check it out!! Your website is up.



The hard way

This is for those folks who think getting their life in some trouble is worthy.
